The Beatles

About

The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. Made up of members John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. One of the most recogniseable bands of all time and the most influencial on their given genre.

Genre

The Beatles originated as a skiffle group, which is a genre with influences from jazz, blues and American folk. The Beatles also embraced 1950s rock and roll and helped pioneer the Merseybeat genre, which is a light, highly melodic style of Beat music. Their repertoire expanded to include a broad variety of pop music which they became quite well known for.

Controversy

Throughout the bands career the Beatles have had their share of controversy. During a tour of the Phillipines they accidentally snubbed the first lady, who expencted them for breakfast due to different cultures it was considered offensive to turn down the offer and resultd in riots and the band escaping the country with some difficulty due to the danger posed to them.

Another large controversy was also due to comments made by John Lennon about Christianity.
"Christianity will go," Lennon had said. "It will vanish and shrink. I needn't argue about that; I'm right and I will be proved right. Jesus was alright but his disciples were thick and ordinary. It's them twisting it that ruins it for me."
This caused heavy backlash from religious groups in America and even in the Vatican.
